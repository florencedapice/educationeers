Project proposal  
Problem: Teachers, admin, and policy makers in Northern New England (NNE) want to engage students in problem-based (PBL) and nature based education (NBE) in order to give them agency, but they feel overworked and lack region-specific resources to do so, and often siloed if they try to start. 

Solution: A website that serves as a hub for teachers and educational organizations to share success stories and region-specific resources they use and offer for PBL and NBE and collaborate on projects.

Audience: Teachers, environmental educators at local organizations, school administrators, parents, students, activists, local and state policy-makers, and academics. 

Included on the website:

1. Academic research on the benefits of PBL and NBE   
   1. Local and global studies (like SustainableED)  
2. Highlighting curricular projects around PBl and NBE in NNE   
   1. Classes being taught in schools, Gardens and outdoor classrooms, Field trips, Traveling expert educators  
3. Highlighting extra-curricular projects in NNE   
   1. Clubs, Internships, Camps   
4. Resources for curricular projects   
   1. Class curriculums and standards  
   2. Outside organizations that can come in and help teachers  
      1. Experts who can consult/chat with schools  
   3. PBL and NBE policy examples for school boards and states  
   4. Grant funding for projects from organizations or government sources  
5. Resources for extra curricular projects  
   1. Project templates and timelines  
   2. **Grant funding sources (orgs and gov)**  
   3. Experts who can come talk to clubs  
   4. Job/internship opportunities for camps or outdoor ed in general

Goals:

1. Replicate other subjects/topics (maybe computer science like [code.org](http://code.org)) that have used this model of loosely connected curriculum support across states/districts  
   1. Convincing both teachers/admin/policy makers to include in the curriculum  
2. Make this helpful to teachers/administrators if they feel overworked already  
   1. Give them less work and not more
